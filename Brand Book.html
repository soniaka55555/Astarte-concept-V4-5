00Logo Marks

Two marks. One brand.

Astarté uses a primary circle lockup for hero and iconic moments, and a horizontal wordmark for navigation, footers, and any horizontal context where the circle would crop awkwardly. Both are equally canonical — never recreate, never approximate, never substitute a typographic stand-in.

Primary · Circle lockup

Use the circle lockup at cover scale — investor deck title slides, About page hero, trailer end card, packaging, Instagram avatar. Minimum reproduction: 80px digital, 16mm print.

Secondary · Horizontal wordmark

Use the horizontal wordmark in navigation bars, footers, email signatures, and any horizontal lockup. Minimum reproduction: 20px digital, 6mm print.

Mark rules · binding

Clear space equals the cap-height of the "a" on all four sides. Never recolor outside the brand palette — white-on-dark and black-on-white only. Never stretch, skew, rotate, or outline. The red accent on the "é" stays red across all uses; this is intentional brand color, not a stand-in.

01Brand Thesis

Heritage. Recognition. Celebration.

Astarté is the first fully inclusive makeup line. Period. Founded 1970. Returning 2026 — reformulated, performance-tuned, and built for the customer who has waited decades to be recognized.

01 · Heritage
First fully inclusive makeup line. Period.

Founded in 1970 by Mari Aldin, with Pat Evans as the original face and Ruby Dee on the 1970 voice-over tape — formulated for every skin tone before there was a word for it. Featured in Poetic Justice (1993). Verified by Columbia Chemistry Lab. Heritage credibility is the moat.

02 · Recognition
Seen. Matched. Understood.

BIPOC consumers spend 30% more than the US beauty average, and are 3× more dissatisfied with their purchases. Recognition is the emotional payoff every other inclusive brand has chased since Fenty.

03 · Celebration
Love my shade. Love Astarté.

The brand's voice warms in body copy — "love my shade," "embrace your skin color," "makeup that loves every skin tone." Heritage frames the headline; celebration carries the day-to-day.

02Legacy Headline & Tagline

Both are locked. The legacy headline anchors heritage moments — investor decks, press, brand book. The tagline carries day-to-day marketing. Neither is to be paraphrased or softened.

03Voice Rules

Plain, confident, never decorative.

Astarté speaks plainly, with technical confidence, and with the assumption that the customer is intelligent. No goddess language. No shade-count headlines. No apologetic framing.

Critical formula language

Astarté has been reformulated for 2026 — it is not the same formula bottled again. Use "performance is the same" when speaking to legacy customers. Never use "same formula," "original formula returns," or "the formula returns" — those phrases are inaccurate and create a regulatory problem.

Terminology split

Marketing surfaces
Makeup

Homepage. PDP. Collection. Email. Trailer. Social.

Investor surfaces
Cosmetics

Pitch decks. Term sheets. Legal. Registered marks.

Words to always use

  • Weightless — performance feel
  • Second skin — finish description
  • Clean performance — formula category
  • Keeps up with you — lifestyle range
  • Lightweight — texture
  • Color-complementing — technology marker
  • 21-free — formulation claim
  • Oil-free, talc-free, fragrance-free, paraben-free
  • Hypoallergenic, gentle for sensitive skin

Words to never use

  • Full coverage — wrong category positioning
  • Heavy — opposite of the formula
  • Thick — opposite of the formula
  • Same formula — inaccurate post-reformulation
  • Original formula returns — inaccurate
  • The formula returns — inaccurate
  • Cosmetics — in any marketing context
  • Goddess — in any customer-facing copy
  • "Luxe / babe / girl / queen"

05Typography

Albert Sans — three weights.

A single family across the brand. Body must be Medium (500) — not Regular. Regular fails accessibility at small sizes on dark backgrounds. This is binding, not stylistic.

Line spacing · binding

Line spacing is tighter than typical luxury beauty defaults. No loose, airy pacing. Body line-height: 1.5–1.55. Display line-height: 0.95–1.1. Small text must not feel airy or weightless on black.

08Heritage Lineage

Five decades. One claim no one else can make.

Astarté's heritage is its uncopiable moat. Every other inclusive brand has had to catch up. The lineage is built from documented moments — not invented mythology. The brand book treats this lineage as the dominant emotional asset of the relaunch.

1970
Mari Aldin founds Astarté.

The first fully inclusive makeup line. Period. Pat Evans is the original face on the 1970 packaging; Ruby Dee delivers the 1970 voice-over (audio archive located May 14, on 7” magnetic tape).

1970s
Outsold Revlon at retail — at twice the price.

First independent beauty brand stocked in U.S. military PXs. Word of mouth across generations of BIPOC consumers who finally found a match.

1993
Featured in Poetic Justice.

A cultural artifact without a press strategy. Cited in the textbook-of-record as a pioneer of inclusive cosmetics.

2000s
A dormant period.

The category moved forward, borrowing without acknowledgment. The formula remained in archive — performance unchanged.

2026
Astarté returns. Reformulated. Performance is the same.

15ml mini in 23 shades, available now. The full 30ml returns later this year. Direct-to-consumer, tech-forward, investor-backed.

How heritage shows up

The lineage lives in the About / Origins page as a chronological scroll, in the homepage as a Sold-Out-Since-the-70s reframe, in the PDP as a single dated reference line, and in the trailer as the final dated card. Verified by Columbia Chemistry Lab is the locked credibility marker that travels with every heritage reference.

09Homepage Rules

Editorial. Dark. Vignette-driven.

Each section is a vignette with its own narrative beat. Dark background dominant. Imagery leads, copy supports. Reference architecture: Nike (immersive carousel) + Casper Caviar (parallax footer).

Do

  • Lead with editorial imagery of diverse women in life moments
  • Use the immersive carousel — 4 panels with rotating content
  • Apply the four locked carousel taglines (May 5 Mindy drafts)
  • Dark background dominant — Hero Black as primary surface
  • Reference Nike homepage architecture — immersive, motion + static
  • Reference Casper Caviar parallax footer — captivating, not dense
  • Treat each section as a vignette with its own beat

Do not

  • No full-size product bottle anywhere on the homepage — JT fully against
  • No mini bottle on homepage — mini appears only on PDP at point of purchase
  • No "girly girly" aesthetic — pink elevated, never childish
  • Do not use the word "cosmetics" in homepage copy
  • Do not use "same formula" or "original formula returns" — inaccurate
  • Do not make the homepage text-heavy — tight line spacing
  • No traditional Sephora-style retail visual conventions
  • No heavy cosmetics-industry visual cues
